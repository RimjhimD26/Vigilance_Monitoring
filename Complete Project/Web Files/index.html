Know Your Passenger Rights

As passengers, we rely on taxi services to take us safely to our desired destination. However, despite the convenience and comfort that taxi rides provide, there are often instances where passengers face issues such as overcharging, unsafe driving practices, and poor treatment. It is important that passengers are aware of their rights so they can protect themselves and take appropriate action if necessary. Below are some Rights a Passenger must know inorder to Ensure their safety and services

Requesting to stop the car

Passengers have the right to request the driver to stop the car if they feel that their safety is at risk. Driver Should immediately stop.

Requesting a change in route

Passengers have the right to request a change in route if they feel that the driver is taking an unsafe or inefficient route.

Privacy

Passengers have the right to privacy while riding in a taxi. Drivers should not share any personal information or conversations that take place during the ride.

Quality of Service

Passengers have the right to courteous and respectful treatment from drivers. Drivers should assist passengers with their luggage and provide a safe and comfortable ride.

Vehicle Condition

The vehicle should be clean, well-maintained, and in good working order. The driver should ensure that the brakes, lights, and other safety features are functioning properly.

Reporting the driver

Passengers have the right to report the driver to the taxi company or regulatory authority if the driver is driving unsafe or is violating the passenger's rights.

No overcharging

Passengers have the right not to be overcharged for the ride any additional charges should be clearly communicated to the passenger.

Identity of the driver

passengers can ask to see the driver's identification card. If a passenger has any doubts about the driver's identity or credential they can deny the ride.

Non-discrimination

You have the right to be picked up and transported regardless of your race, gender, religion, or sexual orientation. Discrimination of any kind is not acceptable.

Contacting emergency services

Passengers can contact emergency services such as police or ambulance if they feel unsafe during the ride

Accessibility

Passengers with disabilities or with any physical Impairment have the right to accessible vehicles and assistance from drivers in loading and unloading their mobility aids.

Digital payment

Passengers have the right to pay for their ride using digital payment methods, such as credit/debit cards or mobile payment apps or even the cab online payment system.

About Application

Vigilance Monitoring for Safer Driving and Passenger Protection

When we enter a Cab or any taxi, we place our trust in the driver to get us safely to our destination. However, as much as we would like to believe that all drivers are responsible and reliable, there have been instances where drivers have posed a threat to the safety and well-being of their passengers. In light of such incidents, vigilant monitoring of drivers has become a crucial aspect of passenger protection. By implementing strict monitoring measures, we can ensure that drivers are held accountable for their actions and that passengers can feel safe and secure during their taxi rides. In this Application, we have put our efforts to meet the importance of vigilant monitoring of drivers and the various measures that can be taken to ensure passenger protection.
